Question Of The Week: Internet scams

  • Internet scams are more and more common and can be tricky to spot if you are unaware of the techniques used by these criminals. I hope to help further equip you in this article by letting you know the techniques fraudsters use in the 5 most common scams so you can tell if someone is trying to trip you up.
  1. Crypto Fraud
    2.
    • Crypto currencies, like Bitcoin, have become more and more prevalent in online scams. One scam that is becoming more and more common are fake cryptocurrency investing platforms. The way this scam works is that a scammer creates an app that appears to be a legitimate investing platform, which convinces victims to put money on the website, and then the website appears to actually generate a return on your money, heck, it may even allow you to withdraw some of it, but this is all done in order to convince the user to put more and more money on the website, until the victim eventually realizes the whole thing was a scam. Often times victims are convinced to put money on these platforms by deepfake videos of celebrities like Elon Musk.   2. Spear Phishing
    3.
    • Spear phishing is a special type of phishing attack that specifically sets out to trick a particular person or group. For example instead of sending a mass phishing email with generic aspects like referring to you as 'user' or simply using just some of your basic information like your first and last name, a scammer may perform more in depth research on a particular person and may use additional information that they have obtained in various ways, such as an attack on the company you work, by performing attacks on people close to you, or just by using the the internet to find your info.
    • To see just how devastating this type of attack can be, lets go back to February and look at a finance employee in Hong Kong who paid out over $25 Million American dollars to someone who had tricked them with spear phishing techniques. The victim was initially suspicious that an email they had received was a phishing attempt, but he was tricked when he joined a video call with the scammer, who was using a deepfake of the CFO. The victim was then convinced and sent $200 million Hong Kong Dollars which is about $25.6 Million American dollars. If you would like, you can read more about this story by clicking here.

  3. Romance Scams
    4.
    • Romance scams are when somebody pretends to be interested in you romantically in order to trick you into giving them money. This happens in many ways, you may find someone on a dating app that seems interested in you, and they build up a relationship with you over time, but eventually, they will find a way to ask for money from you. One way they may do this, which is detailed in The Tinder Swindler on Netflix, is by really building up a relationship with you, including meeting you in person, but then, they will trick you into thinking something bad happened to them, like being injured, and will ask you for money in order to help them out. Another technique scammers use is to attempt to get you to send explicit photos of yourself, and then they will threaten to send them to your friends and family if you don't send them money.

  4. Job Scams
    5.
    • Job scams are when someone offers you a job that seems too good to be true, and it is. One way this scam works is that a scammer will offer you a job that pays a lot of money for very little work, and then they will ask you to pay them a fee in order to get the job. Another way this scam works is that the scammer will offer you a job and then send you a large check which the scammer says is to buy supplies for the job, After you deposit the check and buy the items that they tell you to buy, you will have left over money that you will send back to the scammer. The check will eventually bounce, and you will be out the money that you sent to the scammer.

  5. Service Scams
    6.
    • Service scams are when someone offers you a service in a manipulative way in order to trick you out of your money. One type of service scam, which is detailed in the video below, is where local Facebook groups will be flooded with posts for air duct cleaning services, but there is a middle man involved meaning that you are unaware of the quality of service you will receive. They could be a reliable cleaner or just a random guy blowing air into your ducts, as detailed in a 2013 Ottawa Citizen article.
    • There is another version of this scam where the scammer will have you pay for the air duct cleaning service in advanced, but then they will never show up to clean your air ducts.
    • Another popular service scam is the refund scam. In this scam, the scammer will call you and tell you that you are owed a refund for a service that you have paid for, but they will pretend to give you too much money and ask you to send the extra money back to them. This is often done by remotely connecting to your computer and showing you a fake refund screen, and then they will ask you to send the extra money back to them. This can be seen in the video below.

It is also important to let other people know about new scams that you learn about so that they can also stay protected.
